English Reading Strategy

The project aims to study reading strategy of English learners from different language backgrounds. In the study, you will need to take:
  (a) a language background survey.
  (b) reading materials and comprehension tasks.
Instructions will be given before each task.

Please stay in a quiet place where you won’t be disturbed for at least 15 minutes.

University of California, Los Angeles Linguistics
C140 - Bilingualism and Second Language Acquisition
Yingyu Su, Maria Herrera & Youngseo Kim

Part One: Language Background Survey

Your information will only be used for research purposes.
For the accuracy of the study, please complete the survey honestly.

How would you describe your educational background?

In which way did you mainly learn English?

Have you ever lived in an English-speaking community/country?

How long do you use English every day (i.e. reading, speaking, listening, and writing)

How would you rate your English proficiency?

What kind of English reading are you exposed to in your daily life?

Part Two: reading materials and comprehension tasks

In this part, you will be given 6 very short passages. In three of the passages, the words are purposely transposed, and you are required to try your best to read them as they were normal texts.
Each passage is divided into several pages. You can only read each page once.
It is very important that you click “next” right after you finish reading each page without any pauses.
After reading each passage, some simple comprehension tasks will be given.

You will be given a trial before reading the 6 passages.

Trial

Brian walked up a mountain, and he lost his wooden axe while he was cutting a tree. The axe fell into a nearby pond.

When he was sobbing, the mountain god showed up and asked Brain, “Is this gold axe yours?” Brian said no. The god showed him a silver axe, and asked Brian “Is this yours?” Brian said no.

When the god showed him the wooden axe, Brian said it was his. The god was touched that Brian was so truthful, and gave him the golden axe as a gift.

Q1: What did Brian lose?

Q2: Why did the god gave Brian the golden axe?

You have completed the trial. Just a reminder, it is very important that you click “Next” right after you finish reading each page.
